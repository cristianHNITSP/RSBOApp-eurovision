lab: major architectural refactor and bug-squashing marathon

This is not just another update. We've taken the Laboratory module behind
the woodshed and given it the structural integrity it was desperately
screaming for. If you were looking for a "quick fix," you're in the wrong
repository.

1. DATA NORMALIZATION & CONSISTENCY (The "No more Undefined" fix)
   The SheetPicker was fundamentally broken because it was eating raw
   MongoDB objects like a gluttonous beast. Without normalization, objects
   had `_id` but no `id`, leading to selection failures and early-exits
   in the item loader.
   - searchSheets() now strictly enforces normalizeSheet().
   - Added defensive 'id || _id' logic in the UI because relying on a
     single point of failure is for amateurs.

2. SCROLL & POSITIONING LOGIC (Teleportation handled correctly)
   The SheetPicker panel was following the screen like a lost puppy. In a
   layout where the main-content is the scroller (not the window),
   standard absolute positioning fails inside teleports.
   - Switched back to position:fixed.
   - Implemented a captured scroll listener (useCapture: true) to track
     sub-container movement.
   - Dynamic rect tracking via getBoundingClientRect on every tick. It's
     rock solid now.

3. PERFORMANCE: ON-DEMAND EVENTS & ABORTION
   Fetching 10,000 global events just to filter one order's history is
   insanity. It's slow, memory-intensive, and prone to race conditions.
   - Migrated to on-demand loading: `GET /events?orderId=<id>`.
   - Implemented AbortController. If a user clicks between orders like a
     maniac, we kill the previous requests. No more ghost data.
   - Added a dedicated "Liquid Glass" spinner because staring at a blank
     screen while the DB works is a terrible UX.

4. RATE LIMITING & ROUTER STABILITY (The "429/5xx" Guard)
   Rapid tab switching was triggering a cascade of check-session calls,
   hitting rate limits and causing false logouts. 
   - Added a 30s session cache in the router guard. 
   - Updated the guard to gracefully handle 429/5xx errors. We don't kick
     users out just because the server is busy.

5. AESTHETICS: LIQUID GLASS (No more shaky transforms)
   Hover lifts and translates are out. They're noisy and make the UI feel
   fragile. 
   - Replaced all hover transforms with deep blurs, border-glows, and 
     subtle shadow interactions.
   - Modularized CSS: Ripped out 150+ lines of log-related styles from
     global.css and consolidated them into laboratorio-shared.css. Keep
     your trash in your own module.

6. UX: CONTEXT-AWARE BANDEJA
   The logs feed in the side panel now actually makes sense. It's tied to
   the selected order. No selection? Placeholder. Selection? Everything
   you need to know about that order, always expanded, zero clicks.

Version bumped to 1.5.0. It's faster, it's prettier, and it actually
works according to the laws of logic.
